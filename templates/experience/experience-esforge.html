Embedded Firmware Engineer

Esforge · Robotics

Duration: Jul 2023 - Nov 2024

Responsibilities: At Esforge, I played a pivotal role in advancing robotics projects through firmware development, motor control systems, and hardware integration. My key contributions included:

  • Developed a comprehensive understanding of Marlin firmware for Ender 3D printers, customizing it for robotic applications, including motion planning and kinematics for multi-axis systems.
  • Specialized in advanced motor control, focusing on stepper motors (NEMA 17, NEMA 34), implementing microstepping (up to 1/16) with drivers like A4988 and DRV8825 for high-precision positioning.
  • Designed and wrote reusable C++ libraries for motor control on Raspberry Pi Pico and ESP32, leveraging PIO modules on the Pico to achieve deterministic timing for up to four motors simultaneously.
  • Utilized ESP32’s LEDC module for precise PWM control and FreeRTOS for real-time task scheduling, enabling seamless multitasking in robotics applications.
  • Developed firmware for industrial automation systems, including a protein bar pressing machine and a filling machine, optimizing for reliability and throughput under demanding conditions.
  • Designed custom PCBs for motor control systems, integrating feedback mechanisms like encoders to enhance accuracy and implementing fault detection for system reliability.
  • Explored closed-loop control by integrating magnetic encoders (e.g., AS5600) with stepper motors, implementing PID-like algorithms to correct positional errors in real-time.
  • Collaborated with mechanical engineers to optimize motor performance for specific robotic applications, tuning acceleration/deceleration profiles to minimize vibration and wear.
  • Conducted rigorous testing and debugging using oscilloscopes and logic analyzers to ensure firmware stability, achieving 99.9% uptime in deployed systems.

This role significantly enhanced my expertise in robotics firmware, motor control, and embedded systems design. I gained hands-on experience in integrating hardware and software for industrial applications, preparing me for complex automation challenges and fostering a deep understanding of real-time systems.
